Briana Vecchione

I’m a first year IS PhD student at Cornell interested in issues of bias, fairness, and transparency in AI. At the moment, I’m interested in discussions at the intersection of machine learning, causal inference, and technology policy. During my first year, I plan to explore how these subjects apply to issues of inequality and discrimination through my affiliations with AIPP, the Center for the Study of Inequality, MD4SG, and Queer in AI. If we have similar interests, get in touch: bv225 [at] cornell [dot] edu

Most recently, I was a Microsoft Fellow focused on building transparent MVPs for NYC government. My collaborators at Data & Society & BetaNYC are still carrying forward that work.

In the near-term future, I plan to spend some time talking about my research interests to curious audiences. In the past, I’ve been a feature, keynote, or speaker in NYC spaces like Bloomberg, Microsoft, Facebook, Sony, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Feel free to contact me if you’re interested in more on this.

Acknowledgements: My career was sparked by the amazing folks at the Microsoft Research Data Science Summer School and supported by opportunities through the National Science Foundation, ACM Tapia, and Anita Borg Institute.
